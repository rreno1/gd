02

What is Color?

Color is how our eyes and brain perceive light waves. It is not an inherent property of objects, but rather light reflected off surfaces and received by human eyes.

  • Light Spectrum: Human eyes see a narrow band of electromagnetic radiation (approx. 380nm to 740nm).
  • Human Eye Receptors: Cones detect color (Red, Green, Blue wavelengths), and rods detect light value (darkness/brightness).
  • Objects absorb specific colors of light and reflect others. A red apple absorbs all colors except red, which reflects into your eyes.

Refraction: White light dispersing into the visible color spectrum.

03

RGB vs. CMYK Color Models

Graphic designers work with two primary color modes, depending on whether the final layout is displayed on a digital screen or printed with ink.

  • RGB (Additive Color): Used for digital screens (monitors, phones). Red, Green, and Blue light combine. Mixing all three at 100% intensity creates **White light**.
  • CMYK (Subtractive Color): Used for print. Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Key (Black) inks combine on paper. Mixing all three inks creates a muddy dark color, requiring **Key (Black)** for rich shadows.

05

Color Properties: HSL

Every color can be defined and modified by three key attributes: Hue, Saturation, and Lightness (Value).

  • Hue: The position of a color on the 360-degree color wheel (e.g., Red, Blue).
  • Saturation: The purity or intensity of the color. 100% is vibrant; 0% is completely grey.
  • Lightness (Value): How much white or black is mixed in. 0% is black; 100% is white.

07

Color Psychology

Colors trigger psychological reactions, emotional associations, and cultural meanings. Designers use these triggers to communicate without words.

Red

Excitement & Urgency

Passion, danger, energy, hunger, and urgency. Used by fast food and clearance sales to trigger physical stimulation.

Blue

Trust & Security

Stability, security, trust, and calmness. The most preferred corporate branding color. Heavily utilized by banks and social tech corporations.

Yellow

Optimism & Warning

Optimism, warmth, energy, and warning. Grabs attention immediately (caution tape, taxi cabs). High contrast yellow triggers focus.

Green

Growth & Health

Growth, health, tranquility, and nature. Associates with wealth, biology, organic products, and ecological initiatives.

Purple

Luxury & Mystery

Royalty, luxury, wisdom, and spirituality. In branding, it communicates premium quality, intelligence, and mystery.

Black

Power & Sophistication

Sophistication, luxury, power, and minimalism. Standard for luxury brands, high fashion, and premium editorial layouts.

08

Color & Accessibility

Inclusive design requires making layouts legible for color-blind readers. Color alone should never be the only indicator of critical data.

  • Color Blindness (CVD): Affects roughly 8% of men and 0.5% of women.
  • Protanopia / Deuteranopia: Red-green blindness. Users struggle to tell warning red from success green.
  • Solution: Support colors with descriptive shapes, symbols, and typographic weights (e.g., adding checkmarks and cross icons next to colors).
