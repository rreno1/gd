What is Typography?

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed in visual communication.

  • Utility vs. Emotion: It must convey literal information clearly, but its shape, thickness, and structure establish an emotional tone or voice.
  • Good typography guides readers effortlessly; bad typography creates immediate cognitive strain and reading abandonment.
  • In IT, typography is the core layout component of clean, professional User Interfaces (UI).

Hierarchy & Scale

Visual hierarchy structures information. By sizing headings, subheadings, and paragraphs differently, you dictate the scanning order of your readers.

  • Contrast: Make sure H1 headings are significantly larger than body text. Avoid minor size differences that cause visual conflict.
  • Proportion: Establish a scale system (e.g. Type Scale rules like Golden Ratio or Perfect Fourth scales).

Leading, Tracking & Kerning

Spacing dictates typography breathing room. Adjusting the distance between lines, words, and character pairs changes legibility instantly.

  • Leading (Line Height): The vertical distance between baselines. Standard body leading is 1.4x to 1.6x.
  • Tracking (Letter Spacing): The overall spacing between all characters in a block. Tighten large headers; loosen small body tags.
  • Kerning: The targeted adjustment of spacing between two specific letter characters (e.g. A and V).

Typography Spacing Laboratory

Graphic design relies heavily on spacing. Too little leading suffocates the text blocks, while too much tracking disconnects the individual letter groupings entirely.
07

Alignment & Line Length

Line length determines how far the eye has to travel across a column. Spacing it incorrectly creates severe scanning fatigue.

  • The Golden Rule: Keep body lines between **45 and 75 characters** (including spaces).
  • Lines that are too wide tire the neck and make finding the next line difficult.
  • Lines that are too narrow break semantic word flows, distracting the reader.

08

Typographic Pairings

Combining fonts requires balancing contrast and compatibility. Avoid pairing two fonts that look similar (creating visual conflict).

  • The Golden Rule: Limit a composition to **2 typefaces** (or 3 maximum).
  • Pair a high-personality **Display/Serif header** with a highly legible **Sans-Serif body**.
  • Ensure typefaces share similar x-height structures or proportions to harmonize.

09

Accessibility & Legibility

Inclusive digital products require clear typography sizes, ratios, and spacing variables to support users with low vision.

01

16px Minimum

Standard desktop body font size should never fall below 16px to prevent reading squint.

02

1.5x Line Height

Ensure body copy leading is at least 1.5 times the font size to prevent overlapping lines.

03

Limited Widths

Keep body block widths under 80 characters wide to avoid reading row navigation fatigue.

04

Contrast Ratios

Text must maintain a 4.5:1 ratio against backgrounds (AA rating) for high readability.
