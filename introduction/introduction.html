What is Graphic Design?

Graphic design is more than just making things look pretty. It is the structured practice of planning and projecting ideas and experiences with visual and textual content.

"Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works."

— Steve Jobs

The Elements of Design

These are the fundamental building blocks of any visual composition. Think of them as the vocabulary of visual language.

01

Line

Connects points, guides the eye, defines boundaries, and suggests motion or stability.

02

Shape

2D areas defined by boundaries (geometric, organic, or abstract).

03

Color

Evokes emotion, creates emphasis, establishes identity, and guides visual hierarchies.

04

Value

The lightness or darkness of a color. Essential for creating depth and contrast.

05

Texture

The tactile quality of a surface, either physical or implied visually.

06

Space

Whitespace

The area around or between elements (positive and negative whitespace).

04

Principles of Composition

If elements are the vocabulary, the principles are the grammar rules. They dictate how elements are arranged to build a message.

  • Balance: Distribution of visual weight (symmetrical or asymmetrical).
  • Contrast: Arrangement of opposite elements to draw the eye.
  • Hierarchy: Arranging elements to show order of importance.
  • Repetition / Unity: Reusing elements to build consistency and harmony.

Visual Demonstration

Symmetrical balance distributes weight evenly along a central axis, creating stability and formality.

05

Typography: The Voice of Text

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed.

Leading (Line Height): The vertical space between baselines. Too tight suffocates reader; too loose disconnects lines.
Tracking & Kerning: Tracking is overall letter-spacing. Kerning is space adjustments between specific letter pairs.
Hierarchy: Directs readers. H1 (Primary focus), H2 (Sub-points), Body (Detail content).

06

Color Theory & Harmonies

Colors speak a silent language. They define tone, set moods, and trigger psychological reactions in milliseconds.

Analogous: 3 colors side-by-side on the wheel. Highly serene and cohesive.
Complementary: Opposite colors. High contrast, high energy, bold statements.
Triadic: 3 evenly spaced colors. Balanced, vibrant, and rich.

Grids: The Design Skeleton

A grid is an invisible structure of lines used to align and organize content. It brings order, alignment, and rhythm to a canvas.

Margins

The empty border space framing the canvas content.

Columns & Gutters

Vertical lanes of content, separated by spacing gaps (gutters).

Rule of Thirds

Overlaying a 3x3 grid; aligning elements to intersection points naturally commands attention.

08

Contrast & Accessibility

Good design is inclusive design. Contrast ensures readability, hierarchy, and accessibility for everyone, including visually impaired readers.

WCAG 2.1 Guidelines

  • Normal Text (under 18pt): Requires a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1.
  • Large Text (over 18pt or bold): Requires a contrast ratio of at least 3.0:1.
  • Contrast applies to text, icons, buttons, and form boundaries.
