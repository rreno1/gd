What is a Grid?

A grid is a network of horizontal and vertical lines used to structure content. It acts as the skeleton of a design, providing order, alignment, and predictability.

  • History: Grids date back to early manuscript printing (Gutenberg Bible), but were codified in the 1950s by Swiss designers (Josef Müller-Brockmann).
  • Consistency: Ensures that elements look cohesive across multiple page templates.
  • Speed & Code: For programmers, a solid grid simplifies front-end layouts (CSS Grid, Flexbox, Bootstrap).

The 12-Column Web Grid

The 12-column grid is the standard for web layouts. Why 12? Because 12 is highly divisible by 2, 3, 4, and 6, enabling versatile column combinations.

05

Modular Grids

Modular grids introduce both horizontal and vertical alignment divisions, forming distinct cells (modules). Ideal for dashboards, calendars, and structural galleries.

  • Modules: Individual rectangular units of space.
  • Zones (Fields): Groups of modules merged to form larger content spaces (e.g. merging 4 cells to display a large image card).

Hierarchical Grids

Unlike column or modular grids, hierarchical grids are asymmetric and built directly around the proportions of content items. Common in creative agency portfolios, newsletters, and editorial layouts.

Rule of Thirds & Intersections

Overlaying a 3x3 grid coordinates composition. Placing your subject focal points directly on the grid line intersections creates natural balance and tension.

  • The human eye naturally scans intersection areas rather than the dead center.
  • Usage: Align headers or call-to-action buttons to the rule of thirds grids.

Responsive Grid Break-points

On modern screens, grids must be flexible. Using CSS media queries, layouts collapse columns dynamically as screen viewports shrink.

  • Desktop (Break-point > 1024px): 3 or 4 columns wide.
  • Tablet (Break-point 768px - 1024px): Collapses to 2 columns to keep content readable.
  • Mobile (Break-point < 768px): Columns stack vertically into a single column.

CSS Grid vs. Flexbox

Front-end coders rely on two layout engines. Picking the right tool depends on whether your design axis is 1D or 2D.

  • CSS Grid (2D Layouts): Aligns elements along **both rows and columns** simultaneously (e.g. page shells, image grids).
  • Flexbox (1D Layouts): Aligns elements along a **single axis** (either row OR column, e.g. navbar items, buttons inside forms).
